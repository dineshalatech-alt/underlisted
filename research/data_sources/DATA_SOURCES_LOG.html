The plan to give the whole-USA the best data · what we have · what we can have · what it costs · 2026-06-14 · by Scout

Our goal: the best data system covering the whole USA, so a customer in any state opens Underlisted and finds enough homes — with rich, trustworthy detail. You're open to paying for sources that genuinely add value. This log is the running plan to get there.
How cost works here (so paying stays smart): Underlisted uses ONE shared cache. The worker fetches each property once; every customer reads the cache. So a paid source's cost scales with how many homes we cover (we cap that per city), not how many customers visit. Paid data stays predictable.

2. The honest gaps (to be truly whole-USA + rich)

  • Listings depth & freshness — RentCast is our only listings feed. The most complete/fresh listings live in the MLS (gated) or ATTOM (paid).
  • Value accuracy — RentCast's AVM is fine; HouseCanary is best-in-class and sharpens our core promise (the Deal Score).
  • Property records depth — tax history, owner, deeds. ATTOM is the deepest single API.
  • Climate-risk moat — free FEMA is area-level; First Street gives property-level flood/fire — a stronger differentiator.

5. 🔒 MLS — the gold standard for listings (future)

The freshest, most complete for-sale listings come from the MLS via Bridge Interactive (Zillow Group — API is free), Trestle (CoreLogic), or MLS Grid. Catch: each needs an MLS membership / data agreement (usually a broker relationship + ~$20–50/mo feed dues per MLS + paperwork). Biggest listings-quality jump, but a project — best once we're earning. Not blocking now.
