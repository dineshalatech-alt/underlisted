Underlisted — Strategy & Optimization Map

How the business works · where we optimize · how we win · 2026-06-14 · Atlas + Serena + Scout

1. The goal

Underlisted tells a normal person which U.S. home is actually a good deal — and whether they can truly afford it — in plain English. Nationwide. One Deal Score, the true monthly cost, the real cash needed, and a fire/flood insurance-risk warning. For people who want to live in the home, not flip it.

2. The map — how it works & where we optimize

Red arrows = the live flow. Gold tags = where we invest to optimize. The point: the expensive part (live data) is already built — so our biggest wins are cheap to add.

3. How we win over the other businesses

Every serious competitor is built for investors (cap rate, BRRRR, motivated sellers, jargon) or is market-level. Nobody serves the normal first-time buyer in plain English. That lane is wide open — so we don't out-investor them, we own the lane they abandoned.

4. The moat we build next

"Can I Afford It?" + Surprise-Cost. Enter income, cash, and debts once → every home shows a green/amber/red affordability badge ("you'd have $420 left each month") plus the budget-killers nobody warns about (insurance range, property tax, PMI, HOA), and a plain "why this score" in 3 bullets. No competitor does this, and it's cheap for us — the expensive part (live data + true monthly cost) is already built. It turns our Deal Score from generic into personal to you — a real reason to subscribe.

5. Optimization priorities (the gold arrows, in words)

1. Build "Can I Afford It?" — highest-leverage moat; mostly logic on data we already pay for. (Atlas leads the build.)
2. Deepen the data — RentCast (upgrade) now; ATTOM for depth + First Street for the climate-risk moat, when budget allows.
3. In-house deal videos — "find out if you can afford that house in 30 seconds" → cheap nationwide reach, lower cost per customer.
