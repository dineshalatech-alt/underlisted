🔌 How Underlisted Connects

The plain-English theory of how all your websites work together.

For Dinesha · Underlisted (underlistedhomes.com)

🏪 Big idea: you're opening a shop

Building an app online is like opening a shop. One person can't do everything, so you hire specialist helpers. Each website you signed up for is one helper with one job. Your app is the manager that tells them all what to do.

🔑 What is a "key"?

A key is just a secret password that one helper gives you, so your app can prove "I'm allowed to talk to you." Think of it like a VIP badge 🪪.

  • RentCast says: "Here's your badge. Show it every time you ask me for home data."
  • Resend says: "Here's your badge. Show it every time you send an email."

Your app shows the right badge → the helper says "OK, you're allowed!" and does the job. Without the key, the helper says "I don't know you — go away." That's why nothing works until the key is in place.

📦 Why we hide keys in a "secret box"

If a bad person got your keys, they could pretend to be you (send emails, run up your bills). So we keep keys in a locked secret box:

  • On your computer → the .env file
  • In the cloud → Streamlit "Secrets"
🔒 Golden rule: never paste a key into a chat, email, or public place. It's like shouting your house-key code in the street.

🔗 How two websites actually "connect"

It's always the same 3 steps:

  1. The helper gives you a key — you copy a long secret code from their website.
  2. You put that key in your app's secret box (Streamlit Secrets).
  3. Your app shows the key every time it talks to that helper, over the internet.

That's it. Every connection — RentCast, Resend, the database — works this exact way. 🎯

🗄️ The database key (DATABASE_URL) is special

Most keys are just a password. But DATABASE_URL is an address + password combined — like a note that says: "The filing cabinet is at this building, room 5432, and here's the password to open it."

postgresql:// user : password @ address : port / database (who) (secret) (where) (door) (which cabinet)

So when your app wants to remember something, it reads this note, goes to that exact cabinet, unlocks it, and files the info. 🗂️

🚶 The journey of one customer (it all ties together)

  1. Someone Googles "cheap homes" → finds underlistedhomes.com (the Namecheap address points to the Netlify billboard) 📋
  2. They type their email to join → Netlify saves it on the sign-up sheet ✍️
  3. Later they open your app (running on Streamlit) 👨‍🍳
  4. The app pulls home deals from the Supabase filing cabinet 🗄️ (which the worker filled earlier using RentCast data 🚚)
  5. When a great deal appears, the app tells Resend to email them 📮
Every arrow between those helpers is unlocked by a key 🔑. That's the whole secret.

✅ One-line recap

Each website is a helper with one job. A key is a secret badge that lets your app talk to a helper. You keep keys in a locked box (Secrets). Connect everything by giving your app the right keys — and it manages the whole shop for you.
